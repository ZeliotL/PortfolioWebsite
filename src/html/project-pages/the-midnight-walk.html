The Midnight Walk

VR game where you walk the midnight walk as the Burnt one alongside Potboy

Tools used:

Unreal Engine 5, Substance Designer, Substance Painter, Material Maker

Project type & Timeframe:

Commercial project (indie studio), 2 years

What does this showcase?

This shows how i can work in a larger team, have previous experience with long term projects and in a professional environment.
I can also use unreal engine and its various systems like Niagara for effects, blueprints for functionality, animation blueprints for setting up animation states and animation graphs. Shader graphs for creating advanced and optimized shaders. All of this while keeping it to the limits and performance budget of a VR environment


Make sure to check out the official website for an overview of the game!


More in depth

Spitfire

This is an effect and mechanic in the game i worked on, it included effects for the character, the candle, and the spitfire itself. it calculates the height and distance and moves along a curve that adjustes its speed and height. this is so it can avoid obstacles and move in a sensible speed to feel as natural as possible.


Matchlock

The matchlock is an artillery that shoots matchsticks. i worked on the all of the effects, shaders and overall feel of it.

Monster slurping fire

I worked on the functionality and effect of monsters "eating" the fire from lit candles. It gradually grows weaker and eventually goes out with a poof.

Furnace and Gasturbine

These are two puzzle elements, I worked on the effects, shaders, systems and animations for these objects. When you light them up, their movements start revving up and theres heat spewing out of them everywhere.

Eyelid shader

The player can close their eyes in the game, I made a on screen shader that shows some hidden things in the world, and the shader takes into account which eyes you are closing and shows only part of the screen.

Dust particles

This is a flexible dust effect that changes size and amount based on how big the item is and how hard its landing. Everything about this effect is adjustable based on the needs of where it's being used.
